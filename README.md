# Academic & Polyphasic Skill-Up Protocol

## Overview
This repository contains the optimized daily routine and scheduling protocol for a final-semester (8th Sem) BSc student in Naval Architecture and Offshore Engineering (NAOE). 

The routine is strategically designed to balance a high-load academic timetable with independent tech specialization and language proficiency goals.

## Core Objectives
1. **University Academics:** Efficiently manage NAOE core structural courses and final thesis (NAOE 4000).
2. **Cybersecurity Specialization:** Dedicate 2 solid hours daily to Bug Bounty training.
3. **Language Proficiency:** Dedicate 30 minutes daily to IELTS preparation.
4. **Health & Cognitive Recovery:** Maintain a strict polyphasic sleep schedule (4 hours night / 4 hours day).

## The Polyphasic Sleep Protocol
To accommodate university classes, commuting, and personal skill development, this routine utilizes a segmented sleep schedule:
* **Core Sleep (Night):** 00:00 - 04:00 (4 Hours)
* **Recovery Sleep (Day):** 15:00 - 19:00 (4 Hours)

*Important Note:* Due to extended university classes ending at 14:00 on Tuesdays, Wednesdays, and Thursdays, the day-sleep block is anchored at 15:00 to allow sufficient time for the commute and a meal.

## Daily Time Blocks (Summary)
* **00:00 - 04:00:** Night Sleep
* **04:00 - 09:00:** Morning Deep Work (Thesis, Scripting, Focus Time)
* **09:00 - 14:00:** University Classes (NAOE Meghna Building, Room 402)
* **14:00 - 15:00:** Commute Home & Lunch
* **15:00 - 19:00:** Day Sleep
* **19:00 - 21:00:** Bug Bounty Learning (2 Hours)
* **21:00 - 21:30:** IELTS Preparation (30 Minutes)
* **21:30 - 00:00:** Dinner, Flexibility, & Wind Down

## Execution Rules
1. **Zero Drift Rule:** The 15:00 and 00:00 sleep times are non-negotiable. Do not shift them on weekends, or you will experience sleep-cycle jetlag.
2. **The 19:00 Awakening:** When you wake up at 19:00, your mind is wiped clean of academic fatigue. Capitalize on this by diving directly into the 2-hour Bug Bounty block.
3. **IELTS Micro-Habit:** 30 minutes is short. Do not mix skills in one session. Dedicate specific days to specific modules (e.g., Monday for Listening, Tuesday for Reading, Wednesday for Writing).
